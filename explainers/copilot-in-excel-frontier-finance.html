Copilot in Excel: Built for the Era of Frontier Finance

When AI enters finance, it has to clear finance’s bar: show its work, use trusted data, and trace every calculation. New skills, connectors, and traceability aim to prove it in practice.

SourceMicrosoft 365 Blog
ProductExcel · Copilot

Reading time6 min

The idea in five lines

  • New technology reaches developers first, and finance is often next — and for decades that finance tool has been Excel.
  • AI in finance must clear the same bar: show its work, use trusted data, and trace every calculation.
  • Three new pillars: skills for repeatable workflows, financial connectors for trusted data, and improved traceability.
  • Skills are open-standard SKILL.md files saved in OneDrive that guide Copilot through processes like a DCF or a monthly close.
  • “Plan with Copilot” and change attribution make every edit reviewable — the answer alone isn’t enough; you need to know how you got there.
The concept

Three pillars for finance-grade AI

Copilot capabilities are evaluated across graded levels of task complexity before they ship — in partnership with the Financial Modeling Institute (FMI), whose library of real-world modeling cases is now foundational to how Microsoft tests Copilot in Excel for finance.

Pillar 1 · Skills

Tuned to your standards

Skills let teams define how Copilot should complete common processes — building a DCF, closing the books, refreshing a monthly model, or preparing a variance analysis. Instead of starting from scratch, a skill guides Copilot through the steps and produces an output that’s easier to review, reuse, and trust.

Pillar 2 · Connectors

Grounded in data you trust

Analysis starts from the latest sources instead of manual data pulls. Beyond the LSEG and Moody’s connectors released in May, new financial data connectors bring public and private market data into Copilot in Excel.

ConnectorWhat it brings
CB InsightsPredictive intelligence on private companies and markets — sourcing, emerging-market evaluation, M&A and corp-dev workflows.

Pillar 3 · Control

Controllable by design

In finance, the answer alone isn’t enough — you need to know how you got there. Teams need the same things they’ve always demanded from Excel: visibility into what changed, confidence in the methodology, and a clear trail.

Plan first

Plan with Copilot

Before acting, Copilot outlines which ranges, worksheets, formulas, and assumptions it intends to update — along with clarifying questions.

Trace after

Attribution in Show Changes

Every edit stays traceable, with links back to affected cells, and changes are attributed to Copilot alongside human collaborators.

The result is a Copilot that works more like a trusted analyst: proposing a path forward, explaining its approach, and making every change transparent and reviewable.

Availability

Personalization, workbook rules, pre-built skills, federated connectors, Plan with Copilot, and Copilot attribution are generally available across Excel for Web, Windows, and Mac. Custom skills are in the Insiders channel, rolling to GA next month; partner-built skills arrive Q3 2026.
