Think Through Hard Problems in Voice Mode

Some problems you can’t type your way through. Claude voice mode now runs on Opus and Sonnet, reaches the tools you’ve connected, and speaks many more languages — turning a spoken sounding board into one that can actually act.

The idea in five lines

  • Voice mode used to run only on Haiku — fast, but shallow. Now Opus and Sonnet, the models built for hard problem-solving, are available too.
  • You can switch models mid-conversation, and voice defaults to your last text-chat model, so you move between voice and text without starting over.
  • Voice can now reach your connected tools — Gmail, Slack, Google Calendar, Canva — so a spoken decision becomes an action (with a permission prompt each time).
  • Many more languages are available on every plan — from Hindi and Japanese to Brazilian Portuguese — switchable mid-conversation.
  • It’s in beta on mobile, desktop, and web (best from your phone); Free gets Haiku + one tool, paid plans unlock the bigger models and all connected tools.
Why it changed

A sounding board that keeps up

After hands-free conversation arrived earlier this year, people stopped using voice for quick questions and started working through real business problems out loud, across longer sessions. But voice ran only on Haiku, chosen for speed — it kept things quick, not always deep.

Now Opus and Sonnet are in voice mode. Voice uses the fastest version of whichever model you pick, so conversation still flows. With more capable models you can talk through a half-formed idea and work out what you actually think — Claude asks follow-up questions and builds on your reasoning rather than handing you an answer. It takes turns: listens, pauses to think, then responds.

Some problems you can’t type your way through — practicing a pitch, weighing two offers, reviewing your own process out loud.

What’s new

Three upgrades in one release

Models

Opus & Sonnet in voice

Deeper reasoning for hard problems, not just quick answers. Switch models mid-conversation from the picker; voice defaults to your last text model.

Tools

Reaches your connectors

Voice can now use connected tools like Gmail, Slack, Google Calendar, and Canva — asking permission before each use.

Languages

Many more, on every plan

Talk in the language you think in and switch mid-conversation. Set the language by asking out loud or via the voice language picker.

From talking to doing

When you’ve decided, ask Claude to do it

The upgrade that matters most for knowledge workers: voice is no longer just for thinking. Once you’ve talked something through, Claude can act on it inside your connected tools.
